Professional Summery

I'm a Chemistry PhD graduate, with experience working as a product development chemist.

My first experience with programming was during my PhD: Adapting some Fortran 90 code to implement some new features into a program and developing a different program in Python to carry out calculations and produce graphs.

Recently, and with the help of my mentor, I have been reflecting on my career and remembering how much I enjoyed that work. Further, looking into companies and jobs relating to software development work I believe I would find a career in this area both fulfilling and rewarding.

I’m particularly interested in companies that want to solve problems and make the world a better place. This was what led me to study science in the first place, however increasingly I think I would be more able to contribute to society as a software developer than as a chemist.

My aspiration is to work as a software developer at a company where my work will help people, my initial search suggests that there are a large range of places that could fit this bill. I could stay in a scientific field, developing software to assist research. Or look into broader ways to contribute; in particular I’ve been talking with ThoughtWorks, who are interested in using technology as a tool for social change. I am very interested in working with ThoughtWorks, and it was they who suggested your course.
